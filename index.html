Jump & Landing Protocols

Select the protocol that matches your clinical assessment goals. Each targets different aspects of neuromuscular control and ACL risk.
📦
Drop Jump — 30cm box Subject steps (does not jump) off a 30cm box and lands bilaterally. Standard ACL screening height. Assesses knee valgus at initial contact, hip/knee flexion depth, and bilateral landing symmetry. Recommended starting point for all patients.
📦
Drop Jump — 45cm box Higher-demand version for competitive athletes. The increased drop height amplifies valgus tendencies, making it more sensitive for ACL risk stratification. Use after the 30cm screen shows compensations worth further stress-testing.
⬆️
Drop Vertical Jump (DVJ) Drop from box then immediately perform a maximal vertical jump. Combines eccentric loading with explosive concentric demand. Used in the Hewett et al. ACL injury prediction research. Key metric: knee abduction angle and moment at initial contact.
🦘
Countermovement Jump (CMJ) Standing bilateral jump — rapidly descend then jump maximally. No box required. Assesses reactive neuromuscular power and landing symmetry. Standard athlete monitoring tool. Also used to measure fatigue-related landing quality changes.
🦵
Single-Leg Landing Step or drop off a surface and land on one limb. Direct simulation of the ACL injury mechanism (sudden deceleration, single-leg loading). Highest clinical challenge. Asymmetry in landing strategy is the primary return-to-sport risk indicator. Requires 120fps+ for reliable contact detection.
🏃
Tuck Jump Bilateral jump bringing both knees to the chest. Myer et al. Tuck Jump Assessment (TJA) — 10-item observational checklist. Standard ACL risk screen, particularly validated for female athletes. Key observations: knee valgus, foot separation, asymmetric arm swing, landing stiffness.
↔️
Triple Hop for Distance Three consecutive maximal single-leg hops for maximum distance. Limb Symmetry Index (LSI) standard: LSI >90% is the typical return-to-sport threshold. Record both limbs. MotionRx measures landing mechanics on each contact; distance must be measured manually and entered in clinical notes.
